What Mothers and Childfree
Women Have In Common

Our lives may look different, but we can all relate to this

Elise Lachapelle Follow Nov 10 . 4 min read

Photo by Vlada Karpovich from Pexels

Some time ago, I wrote a deeply personal and brutally honest essay entitled, “I Don’t Want More Kids.” Hitting “publish” and then “share” set my nerves on edge. My social media channels are full of peers who have struggled (or are struggling) to have children, who can’t wait to have more children, or who aren’t having any more children but certainly didn’t stop at two the way my husband and I did. Making such a bold claim right there in the essay’s title, I braced myself for angry tirades and sanctimonious lectures. What I didn’t expect was to be inundated with something else entirely.


Personal stories began pouring in under my Facebook post, through my Instagram DMs, and even via Facebook Messenger. Moms of one fielding questions about having more kids anytime they left the house. Moms of one boy and one girl being told they have the “perfect family.” Moms of two same-sex kids getting asked if they would try for a girl or a boy, whichever one they didn’t have. Moms of three who used to get those questions, but not so much anymore. A mom of four who shared that she still did.
